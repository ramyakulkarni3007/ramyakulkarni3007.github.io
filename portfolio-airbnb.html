Project Description

Airbnb has transformed the hospitality industry by provid- ing a platform for people to rent out their spaces to guests. Our report presents an extensive Airbnb dataset visualization that spans across various cities in the United States. Our innovative approach focuses on a neighborhood-centric analysis, aiming to provide a granular view of the data through the lens of local communities.
The project utilizes a rich set of visualization techniques to analyze and present the data. Among these, the interactive choropleth map is a central feature. Created with Plotly and integrated into a Streamlit dashboard, this map allows users to interactively explore neighborhood ratings and delve into detailed information with ease. Once the neighborhood is clicked on the map additional visuals are presented to the user.
Word Clouds capture the cultural essence of neighborhoods through host descriptions, while Polar/Radar Plots compare property scores like accuracy and cleanliness against city averages.
Histograms and Bar Plots show the distribution of ratings and property types, helping users navigate accommodation options. Parallel Coordinate Plots illustrate the links between superhost status, review scores, capacity, and pricing. To complete the visualization suite, Calendar Heatmaps display the fluctuation of property availability throughout the year, guiding potential guests in finding the best times to book. Finally, an interactive table is presented, enriched with images and filters, serves as a practical tool for users to select properties that best suit their preferences. By clicking on a property, users are directed to the Airbnb website where they can finalize their booking.
This project aims to bridge the gap between data and decision-making, providing an intuitive and insightful resource for travelers, hosts, and market analysts alike.
